38 (3.7.2)
LANDING WITH A DEFECTIVE TIRE ON THE MAIN LANDING GEAR

---
1. CAUTION
2.
3. A defective (e.g. burst) tire is not usually easy to detect. The
4. damage normally occurs during take-off or landing, and is hardly
5. noticeable during fast taxiing. It is only during the roll-out after
6. landing or at lower taxiing speeds that a tendency to swerve
7. occurs. Rapid and determined action is then required
8. 1. Advise ATC
9. 2. Land the airplane at the edge of the runway that is located on the side of the intact
10. tire, so that changes in direction which must be expected during roll-out due to the
11. braking action of the defective tire can be corrected on the runway
12. 3. Land with one wing low. The wing on the side of the intact tire should be held low
13. 4. Direction should be maintained using the rudder. This should be supported by use
14. of the brake. It is possible that the brake must be applied strongly - if necessary
15. to the point where the wheel locks. The wide track of the landing gear will prevent
16. the airplane from tipping over a wide speed range. There is no pronounced tendency
17. to tip even when skidding
